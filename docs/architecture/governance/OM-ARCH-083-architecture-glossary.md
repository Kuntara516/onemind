# Architecture Glossary

> **A shared architecture begins with a shared language.**

---

# 1. Purpose

This glossary establishes the official architectural vocabulary for the OneMind platform.

Its objectives are to:

- Standardize terminology
- Eliminate ambiguity
- Improve communication
- Ensure consistency across documentation
- Support onboarding
- Preserve conceptual integrity

All architecture documents shall use the terminology defined in this glossary.

---

# 2. Principles

Every term shall:

- Have one official definition.
- Be used consistently across all documents.
- Avoid duplicate meanings.
- Avoid technology-specific interpretations unless explicitly stated.

---

# 3. Core Business Terms

## Business Capability

A stable business ability that delivers value independently of organizational structure, technology, or processes.

---

## Business Service

A service exposed by a business capability to internal or external consumers.

---

## Value Stream

The sequence of business activities that creates measurable value.

---

## Domain

A logical business boundary that owns capabilities, data, and services.

---

## Bounded Context

A well-defined boundary in which a specific domain model applies.

---

# 4. Platform Terms

## Platform

The shared foundation enabling applications, agents, data, workflows, and AI services.

---

## OneMind

An AI Operating Platform that unifies people, knowledge, workflows, assets, and intelligent agents into a single operational mind.

---

## Module

A deployable functional unit within the platform.

---

## Component

A reusable software building block with a clearly defined responsibility.

---

## Service

An independently deployable capability accessible through well-defined interfaces.

---

## Microservice

A small, autonomous service responsible for a single business capability.

---

# 5. AI Terminology

## Agent

An autonomous software entity capable of reasoning, planning, and executing tasks.

---

## Multi-Agent System

A collection of collaborating agents working toward shared objectives.

---

## Planner Agent

Coordinates tasks and delegates work to specialized agents.

---

## Worker Agent

Executes assigned tasks within a defined scope.

---

## Memory

Persistent contextual information available to AI agents.

---

## Short-Term Memory

Context maintained during an active interaction.

---

## Long-Term Memory

Persisted knowledge retained across sessions.

---

## Knowledge

Structured information available for reasoning and decision-making.

---

## Knowledge Base

A managed collection of validated knowledge assets.

---

## RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)

A technique that enhances AI responses using retrieved external knowledge.

---

## Embedding

A numerical vector representation of information used for semantic search.

---

## Vector Database

A database optimized for storing and searching embeddings.

---

## LLM Gateway

A service responsible for routing requests to one or more language models.

---

# 6. Data Terminology

## Master Data

Core business entities shared across the platform.

---

## Transaction Data

Data generated by operational activities.

---

## Metadata

Information describing other data.

---

## Data Lineage

The origin, movement, and transformation history of data.

---

## Data Catalog

A searchable inventory of enterprise data assets.

---

## Knowledge Graph

A graph-based representation of entities and relationships.

---

# 7. Integration Terminology

## API

Application Programming Interface.

---

## REST API

A resource-oriented HTTP interface.

---

## Event

A record that something significant has occurred.

---

## Event Bus

Infrastructure used to distribute events between services.

---

## Message Queue

A mechanism for asynchronous communication.

---

## Webhook

An HTTP callback triggered by an event.

---

# 8. Security Terminology

## Authentication

Verification of identity.

---

## Authorization

Verification of permissions.

---

## Zero Trust

A security model based on continuous verification.

---

## Least Privilege

Granting only the minimum required permissions.

---

## Encryption

Protection of data through cryptographic transformation.

---

## Secret

Sensitive credentials such as passwords, API keys, or certificates.

---

# 9. Architecture Governance

## Architecture Principle

A fundamental rule guiding architectural decisions.

---

## ADR

Architecture Decision Record documenting significant architectural decisions.

---

## Architecture Review

A structured assessment of architectural quality and compliance.

---

## Architecture Board

The governing body responsible for approving significant architectural decisions.

---

## Technical Debt

The future cost incurred by choosing a simpler solution today.

---

## Architecture Drift

Gradual divergence from approved architectural principles.

---

## Architecture Baseline

The officially approved reference architecture at a given point in time.

---

# 10. Deployment Terminology

## Environment

An isolated deployment stage such as Development, Test, UAT, or Production.

---

## Infrastructure as Code (IaC)

Managing infrastructure through version-controlled code.

---

## Container

A lightweight executable software package containing an application and its dependencies.

---

## Orchestration

Automated deployment, scaling, and management of containers.

---

## High Availability

System design that minimizes service interruption.

---

## Disaster Recovery

Processes for restoring services following catastrophic failure.

---

# 11. Observability

## Logging

Recording system events.

---

## Metrics

Quantitative measurements describing system behavior.

---

## Tracing

Tracking requests across distributed services.

---

## Monitoring

Continuous observation of platform health.

---

## Alert

A notification triggered by predefined operational conditions.

---

# 12. Documentation Rules

When introducing a new architectural term:

- Verify that it is not already defined.
- Add it to this glossary.
- Update affected documentation if terminology changes.
- Preserve backward compatibility where possible.

---

# 13. Governance Rules

All official architecture documents shall use the definitions contained in this glossary.

Conflicting terminology shall be resolved by updating this document before approving new architecture documents.

This glossary is the authoritative source for architectural terminology within OneMind.

---

# 14. Definition of Done

A glossary update is complete when:

- New terms are clearly defined.
- Existing definitions remain consistent.
- Related documents are updated if necessary.
- Terminology conflicts are resolved.
- Changes are committed to the repository.

---

# 15. Summary

A common architecture language is essential for long-term platform evolution.

By maintaining a single authoritative glossary, OneMind ensures that architects, developers, AI assistants, and business stakeholders communicate using consistent terminology, reducing ambiguity and preserving conceptual integrity.

---

> **Shared architecture begins with shared language. Shared language enables shared understanding.**
